Accelerating MBSE Adoption for a Complex Product Team

Client Challenge

An engineering organization developing a multi-subsystem product needed to move from document-centric systems engineering to model-based practices. Teams lacked a shared architecture view, and requirements were managed separately from design artifacts.

Consulting Approach

We assessed current processes and toolchain readiness, defined an MBSE adoption roadmap, and established initial SysML 2.0 modeling conventions. Working embedded with the systems team, we developed reference architecture models and facilitated workshops to align stakeholders on scope and interfaces.

Deliverables

MBSE adoption roadmap, modeling standards document, reference system architecture model, requirements-to-architecture traceability framework, and team training sessions on SysML 2.0 fundamentals.

Business Impact

The client established a consistent architecture baseline shared across engineering disciplines, reducing ambiguity in early design discussions and creating a foundation for lifecycle traceability as the program matures.

Connecting Requirements, Architecture, and CAD Deliverables

Client Challenge

A product team struggled to maintain alignment between system requirements, architecture decisions, and mechanical design outputs. Changes in one domain were not consistently reflected in others, creating rework and verification gaps.

Consulting Approach

We mapped the existing engineering data flow, identified traceability breakpoints, and defined a digital thread approach linking requirements models to architecture elements and CAD configuration references. Mechanical interface definitions were standardized to support cross-domain coordination.

Deliverables

Traceability matrix templates, interface control documentation framework, CAD-integrated workflow guidelines, assembly design review checklist, and updated configuration management practices.

Business Impact

Engineering teams gained clearer visibility from requirements through to physical design artifacts, improving change coordination and reducing inconsistencies during design reviews.

Improving Verification Planning with Model-Based Traceability

Client Challenge

A program approaching detailed design needed a structured verification approach but lacked traceability between requirements, test cases, and system models. Verification planning was ad hoc and difficult to maintain as requirements evolved.

Consulting Approach

We developed a verification planning framework linked to the existing requirements repository and system models. Model-based verification relationships were defined, and a test architecture was structured to align with system decomposition.

Deliverables

Verification and validation plan, requirements verification matrix, test architecture document, model-based verification guidelines, and compliance evidence collection templates.

Business Impact

The client moved toward a repeatable verification process with traceable evidence, improving confidence in requirements coverage and readiness for design milestone reviews.
